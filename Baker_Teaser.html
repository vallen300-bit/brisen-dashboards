The Problem

CEOs operating at scale — multiple entities, jurisdictions, 20+ active projects — face a structural information problem that no amount of hiring solves.

  • Volume. Hundreds of daily signals across email, WhatsApp, Slack, task managers, calendars, meeting transcripts, and contractor updates. No human filters all of it.
  • Fragmentation. Critical context is split across 10+ platforms. The deadline that matters is in one system, the related email in another, the relevant contract in a third.
  • Role conflict. The CEO must be in the trenches — handling fires, responding to urgent stakeholders, making real-time calls. But he must simultaneously be the general — holding the strategic map, catching the project that's quietly drifting while the loud ones consume attention.
  • Stakeholder load. Investors, employees, counteragents, sales teams, outside contractors — all pulling in different directions, all expecting fast responses. One missed signal cascades.
  • Role erosion. The cumulative effect: the CEO spends 80% of his time managing operations. The role he should be playing — owner, shareholder, strategic allocator — gets squeezed into whatever time is left.

The standard response: more assistants, more meetings, more check-ins. That adds noise, not intelligence. Baker solves this differently. It doesn't add people. It adds a system that lets the CEO stop managing and start owning.

How It Works — Agentic RAG

Most AI tools use basic RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): you ask a question, the system searches a document store, returns a text snippet. Reactive. Single-source. Stateless.

Baker uses Agentic RAG — a fundamentally different architecture:

  • Multi-source reasoning. Agents query across email, WhatsApp, calendar, ClickUp, Todoist, meeting transcripts, and RSS simultaneously — then cross-correlate.
  • Autonomous action. Baker doesn't just retrieve. It reasons, decides, and acts — posting comments, generating alerts, drafting responses, updating deadlines — without waiting for a prompt.
  • Persistent memory. Every interaction, decision, and deadline stored in a knowledge graph. Baker gets smarter over time. Basic RAG resets every session.
  • 11 specialist agents. Not one generalist model. Domain-specific agents for finance, legal, operations, sales, marketing, product management, customer support, data analysis, enterprise search, compliance, and strategic planning — each trained on professional methodology.

Basic RAG answers questions. Agentic RAG runs operations.
